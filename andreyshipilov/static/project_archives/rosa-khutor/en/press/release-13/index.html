Rosa Khutor to Support WWF Project, Aimed at Reintroducing the Persian Leopard in the Northern Caucasus.

World Wildlife Fund (WWF) and Rosa Khutor launched a project, supported by the Russian Ministry of Nature Resources and Russian Federal Natural Resource Oversight Agency (Rosprirodnadzor), aimed at reintroducing the Persian leopard in the Northern Caucasus. The project will become the first WWF-Russia conservation project that is mostly sponsored by Russian businesses, as it is sponsored not only by Rosa Khutor, but also by Russian mobile operator Beeline.

The Persian leopard once inhabited almost all of the Caucasus. However, due to intense extirpation the population declined drastically in late 19th – early 20th centuries. Severe winters (deep snow cover, small numbers of hoofed) and ongoing pursuit by people resulted in all but extinction of leopards in 1960–1970. Only a few leopards now inhabit the most remote places of the Russian Caucasus.

The aim of the project proponent is to return the Persian leopard in Russia. That involves transportation of animals from several European zoos, building of pens for them, adaptation of animals to the Caucasus natural environment.

“We are glad that our idea of reintroducing the leopard in the Russian Caucasus has received support, support both from scientific community and the government and from the Russian business at the expense of which the project will be mostly funded”, says Igor Chestin, WWF-Russian Director.

“We were attracted by the uniqueness of this project”, said Nina Zagorulko, director general of Rosa Khutor. “Previously, there were no such projects for reintroduction of the vanishing species in the world practice. And we like the idea to lead not only in business or sports. Besides, the presence of leopards is believed to be a sign of a healthy region’s ecosystem. And this is very important for the development of ecotourism. All this complies with the concept, adopted by Rosa Khutor”, she added.

The main arrangements through the program:

2006–2013 – hoofed restoration in the reintroduction area (carrying out of bio-technical measures (supplementary feeding, creating of solonetzs), strenthening of protection from poachers),

2006–2007 – forming groups of founders of nature populations in captivity (there will be established a Breeding Center with pens for animals transported from European zoos in the territory of the Sochi National Park),

2007–2008 – adaptation of animals for wildlife (there will be created several pens in the areas where animals will be released for baby leopards transported from the Breeding Center to learn wildlife rules),

2008–2013 – release of leopards into the wild.
