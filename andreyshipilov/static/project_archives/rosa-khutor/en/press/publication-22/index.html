Moskovsky Komsomolets

Olympic projects have gone up in the world

Sochi seashore — zero cycle

The Coordination Commission of the International Olympic Committee (IOC) has completed its work in Sochi. For two days the important guests have been assessing what had been done since their first visit to the Black Sea resort at the end of last April.

Though, this time the IOC Commission spent more time in conference, than inspecting. Beyond the closed doors of luxury hotel Rodina, meetings with the Vice Prime Ministers of the RF Government Dmitry Kozak and Alexander Zhukov were held on 13 and 14 May, as well as with the President of the organizational Committee Sochi-2014 Dmitry Chernyshenko. Besides, already after the final press conference the foreign VIP persons were to have a business dinner with the Prime Minister of RF Vladimir Putin.

By the way, it was here that the main news on the subject of preparation for the XXII Winter Olympics originated. Putin suggested making three of the five ice stadiums dismountable, in order to relocate them subsequently to other regions, where winter sports are not sufficiently developed. VVP definitely does not want the Sochi arenas to repeat the destiny of the famous “Bird’s Nest” in Beijing. The main “jewel” in the crown of Olympics-2008 is simply playing idle, whereas its maintenance still requires substantial funding.

But for this idea of Putin’s the journalists would have been bored to death. Like a year ago, the Chairman of IOC’s Commission Jean-Claude Killy expatiated in praise of the Russian side. His speech was tagged with such phrases as: “great progress has been accomplished”, “all works are being completed on time”, “there are no faults”. In the end the famous ex-skier promised to visit Sochi within 6 weeks’ time by the beginning of the construction of several ice stadiums in the flat part of the city.

Dmitry Chernyshenko described the existing state of things in a rather quaint manner: “Today we have moved from the stage of strategic planning to operational planning”. But Dmitry Kozak was more straightforward: “The construction of the main Olympic projects will be completed in the year 2012”. Alexander Zhukov had tallied all the expenses in the same concise manner: “The budget of the Organizational Committee Sochi-2014” will be 1.8 billion dollars, of which only 330 million will be provided by the state. The rest will be provided by the sponsors”. Killy himself finally calmed down the foreign reporters by saying that in terms of direct costs the Sochi Games are comparable with Turin-2006 and Vancouver-2010.

The press-conference participants were not daunted by other sharp questions. For example, the absence of Victor Kolodyazhny, the Head of the State Corporation Olympstroy, was explained by his vacation. When Killy was asked about his attitude to frequent changes of mayors in Sochi, the Frenchman made a pint of praising the recently elected mayor Anatoly Pakhomov, and then he changed the conversation to a philosophic plane, purporting that life was life, and there was nothing to be done about it.

Dmitry Kozak talked on the subject of resettlement of the residents from Imeretin Valley where the Olympic Park would be built: “As of today, 70 preliminary agreements have been signed,” said the Vice Prime Minister. “At the end of this year all these people will receive new housing in the village of Nekrasovka, and another 25 families have received money compensation.”
Generally speaking, all the snags were successfully dealt with. Let us see what will happen when the time comes to show real construction sites instead of models and computer films.
In this respect the skiing complex Rosa Khutor has so far been the leader. The cable road is already operating, together with 6 meteorological stations, which provide on-line all the necessary information about the air temperature and the depth of the snow cover. There is another fact: at the end of January, IOC members bowled down the men's downhill skiing slope, AS a result, a decision was made to hold a European Skiing Cup in Rosa Khutor in 2011.

By Butnev Y.
