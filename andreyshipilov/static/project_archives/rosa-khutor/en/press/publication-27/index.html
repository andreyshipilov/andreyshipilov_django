Komsomolskaya Pravda

Krasnaya Polyana: five years to the Olympics


KP has tested Sochi’s mountain pistes.

What has changed in a year since the time when I first went downhill on the Olympic slopes (see KP of 28 February, 2008)? The construction in Adler is developing on a large scale. On the Black Sea shore, in the isthmus of Mzymta River, appeared a host of cranes and graders erecting the port, where the building materials for the construction of sports arenas, the main Olympic village, the press center will be delivered in a year’s time…

Several kilometres from the sea, at the edge of Imeretin Valley, in Mirny village the construction of the freight terminal and railway hub is in full swing, from where, by the way, a narrow-gauge railway will be built as far as Krasnaya Polyana. The motor road to the mountains, with a dozen of tunnels, is fully launched. To put it in a nutshell, the foot of the mountain pistes is just within a half-hour car ride from Adler airport.

But Krasnaya Polyana meets you with half-empty ski lifts, deserted newly built hotels… And this is the peak of the season!

The crisis has reached the mountains too. But no-one is going to reduce the prices. The cost of hotel rooms may go up to 8 thousand roubles per night. You will also have to cough up for a dinner in a small restaurant, leaving at least one thousand roubles. What can you say, if a bottle of domestically produced beer in a kiosk costs one hundred roubles? It is three-four times as much as in Moscow. Plus, a pass to the ski lift on a weekday will cost you one thousand roubles. This is why the unique mountains remain deserted…

However, the slopes meet European standards: the pistes are smooth; the ski lifts are not crowded. Except for the fourth one, the uppermost stage, at Alpika Service — after heavy snowfalls it is teeming with free riders, who want to get a chance to whoosh down on the virgin snow. The Gazprom slope, where the President and the prime Minister have been skiing for several years, is working like clockwork. The Rosa Khutor center where alpine skiing competitions will be held during Olympics-2014 is developing quickly. Different courses are already discernible. One of them is prepared by ultra-modern ratracs. Quite recently it has been tested by IOC Commission members. Now it is my turn…

But where are the long-awaited ski lifts, ski houses, alpine restaurants? The will appear soon enough. However, the plans are changing incessantly... In the initial Olympic application Rosa Khutor featured only as an alpine skiing center. But last year, on the insistence of environmentalists, the future Olympic village, where during the Olympics 2,600 skiers will be living, also moved here. It will also be a venue for the biathlon, snowboard and freestyle competitions. In other words, in a couple of years Rosa Khutor will become a world-class alpine skiing resort. I only wish they did not jack up prices. It would also be great if they built a dozen of similar centers around the country. Otherwise, we will end up with our Russian Courchevel for the select ones…

As for the confidence that the Sochi Olympics will be delivered to the highest standards, I have no doubts whatsoever. I am also sure that the entire Caucasus, which is even better for the winter sports than the glorified Alps, will come to life…

BY THE WAY

Within two kilometers of Rosa Khutor, the new center Gornaya Karusel is being completed where it is not planned to lay any Olympic courses, in winter 2014 it will house a journalist village. Within a year already four (!) mountain centers will be operating. And this means something…

By Serov К.
